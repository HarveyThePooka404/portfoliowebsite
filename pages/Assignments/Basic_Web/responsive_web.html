02.04.01 Responsive website

Goal

We were to make our website, made for our give art style, responsive. The changes were meant to be only technical, while not changing the content of the website.


This is the home page

Process

The responsive website you will see was the second version made this semester. The current version of Owldesign.dk (v2) was made during the re-design for “02.03.01 Design, UX and process document”. The first wireframe show that the first version was too vertical, and was not responsive enough. After re-doing the wireframe, I decided to do a high-fidelity prototype on XD. It helped me to follow a clearer and more strict structure. Working with XD offered a better example of what was working.


These are the wireframes

What worked well

Through the whole process, it became clear for me that planning and having a precise idea of the final website would look like was best. I do believe that the simpler the design, the best the website. It was harder to have a smooth, uncrowded version of the website.

The use of breakpoints for the website gave a smoother, better browsing. The user would have had three different experiences for the website, specifically thought for the devices they are on.


The Ipad and Iphone version

What could have been improved

To do the carrousels, I had to use some Javascript - which we had not learn at the time. I lost a lot of time trying to understand everything in the code, while I could have done something similar (or better) in vanilla Html/CSS. It would have been probably better to use Keyframe animation.
